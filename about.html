About us

Built for the person asking the question at 2am

We built the burnout assessment we wished existed — free, private, honest, and designed to help rather than alarm.

Why we built this

Most free burnout quizzes online are either clinically meaningless (5 yes/no questions and a vague result) or require an email address, a paid subscription, or both. The tools built by researchers are rigorous but inaccessible — designed for clinical settings, not for someone sitting at their laptop at midnight wondering why they can't stop crying after a perfectly ordinary Tuesday.

We built CheckMyBurnout to fill that gap: a 20-question assessment informed by validated clinical research frameworks (particularly the Maslach Burnout Inventory dimensions), delivered in a way that's accessible, genuinely private, and focused on actionable self-awareness rather than alarming diagnoses.

What it's for — and what it isn't

This is a self-awareness and screening tool. It's designed to help you get an objective read on where you are across five burnout-relevant dimensions, identify which areas need the most attention, and track your progress over time.

It is not a clinical diagnosis, a substitute for professional mental health support, or a replacement for a conversation with your GP or therapist. If your score is high — particularly if it's accompanied by persistent low mood, thoughts of self-harm, or significant functional impairment — please reach out to a professional.

Privacy

All calculations happen in your browser. Your answers, scores, and weekly history are stored only in your browser's local storage — they never leave your device. We do not collect, store, or transmit any of your assessment data.
